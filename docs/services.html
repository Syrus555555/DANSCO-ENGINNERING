Structural Engineering

We design and analyze structural systems for new construction, renovation, and adaptive reuse across residential, commercial, and industrial sectors — licensed in 30+ states.

From a single-family home addition to a multi-story commercial building, our engineers develop complete structural systems that meet all applicable building codes. We work closely with architects, general contractors, and developers to deliver accurate, constructible, and cost-effective designs on time.

Our work includes everything from early-stage schematic structural concepts to stamped construction documents ready for permit submission.

  • New construction structural design
  • Building renovation & retrofit
  • Foundation and slab-on-grade design
  • Lateral load analysis (wind & seismic)
  • Steel, concrete, wood & masonry systems
  • Connection design and detailing
  • Stamped drawings for permit
  • Engineering letters and certifications
  • Construction phase support
  • Peer review services

What You Receive

  • Stamped & signed structural drawings
  • Structural calculations package
  • Engineering letter / certification (when applicable)
  • Coordination with architect and contractor

Shoring & Formwork Engineering

We engineer shoring systems, concrete formwork, and temporary structures that keep your construction operations safe, code-compliant, and on schedule — from residential pours to high-rise core walls.

Improperly designed shoring and formwork is one of the leading causes of construction accidents and delays. Our licensed engineers provide complete design packages that satisfy OSHA requirements, local building department requirements, and the structural demands of your specific pour sequence and loading conditions.

We work directly with concrete subcontractors and project managers to develop practical designs that can be built quickly and safely in the field.

  • Shoring system design & load calcs
  • Concrete formwork engineering
  • Reshoring & backshoring analysis
  • Elevated slab shoring layouts
  • Wall form and gang form design
  • Slip form & jump form support
  • Temporary structure design
  • OSHA-compliant documentation
  • Shop drawing review & approval
  • Field consultation & RFI response

What You Receive

  • Stamped shoring / formwork design drawings
  • Load calculations and material specifications
  • Pour sequence recommendations
  • OSHA submittal-ready documentation package

Who This Is For

Concrete Subcontractors General Contractors Construction Managers Developers

Truss Engineering & Repairs

We analyze, design, and document repair solutions for wood and metal truss systems — including new designs, storm damage assessments, load modification studies, and full repair plans accepted by insurance carriers nationwide.

Whether you're building new or dealing with damaged trusses after a hurricane, fire, or structural overload, our engineers provide fast, accurate reports that hold up to scrutiny from insurance adjusters, building officials, and courts alike.

We regularly work on truss repairs throughout Florida and the Gulf Coast, where wind events frequently require engineering documentation for insurance claims and re-roofing permits.

  • New truss system design
  • Hurricane & storm damage assessment
  • Repair and remediation plans
  • Load modification evaluations
  • Overstress & overspan analysis
  • Wood truss systems (residential & commercial)
  • Metal plate connected trusses
  • Open web steel joists
  • Insurance documentation reports
  • Litigation support & expert opinions

What You Receive

  • Stamped engineering report with findings
  • Repair detail drawings (when applicable)
  • Load calculations and analysis summary
  • Insurance-ready documentation package

Structural Inspections

Our licensed engineers perform thorough on-site structural condition assessments and deliver written reports suitable for permitting, insurance claims, property transactions, and litigation — typically within 48 hours of the site visit.

A structural inspection from a licensed P.E. carries weight with building departments, insurance carriers, lenders, and courts. We inspect all types of residential and commercial structures, documenting existing conditions, identifying deficiencies, and recommending corrective action when needed.

Our inspections are commonly requested prior to purchasing a property, after storm or fire damage, as part of a building permit process, or when a structure's integrity is in question.

  • Structural condition assessments
  • Pre-purchase due diligence inspections
  • Post-storm damage documentation
  • Load capacity evaluations
  • Foundation & slab inspections
  • Concrete deterioration assessments
  • Roof structure inspections
  • Retaining wall evaluations
  • Insurance claim documentation
  • Expert witness & litigation support

What You Receive

  • Stamped written engineering report
  • Photo documentation of observed conditions
  • Summary of findings and recommendations
  • Repair scope / remediation guidance (when applicable)
